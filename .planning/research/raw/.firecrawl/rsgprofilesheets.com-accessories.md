## CORNER Accessories

CORNERS are important roofing accessories used to cover and protect the joint where two sides of a roof or wall meet, especially at right angles. In the case of profile roofing sheets, corners ensure proper sealing, weather protection, and aesthetic finish at edges.

## Specifications of CORNERS Accessories in Roofing Sheets

Corner accessories in roofing sheets are used to seal and protect the joints at external or internal corners of buildings. They are typically made from galvanized iron (GI), aluminum, or color-coated steel with a thickness ranging from 0.40 mm to 0.80 mm. The standard length is 10 feet, with arm widths 150mm to 1220mm and a 90° bending angle. These corners come with zinc or Alum- zinc paint coatings (like PPGI/PPGL) ensure rust and weather resistance. Available in multiple colors and profiles, they are fixed using screws or rivets and provide a neat, durable, and leak-proof finish to roofing structures.

## Key Features:

- Prevents water leakage at joints
- Gives clean and finished look to the roof
- Provides rust and weather resistance

## Turbo fans

Turbo fans are specifically designed high-performance air ventilation systems that provide adequate airflow throughout industrial and commercial roofing systems. These ventilators, made of high-quality materials, run solely on wind energy and require no electricity. Furthermore, they reduce heat buildup in indoor areas, which improves air quality and comfort in the workplace. These turbo fans are well-built, long-lasting, and weather-resistant, making them an excellent choice for any sustainable roofing solution.

## Metal roof Flashing

Metal roof flashing is a critical component in roofing systems, designed to prevent water penetration at joints, edges, and roof transitions. Made from durable materials like galvanized steel, aluminum, or copper, flashing is strategically installed around chimneys, vents, skylights, valleys, and wall intersections to create a watertight seal. Its primary function is to direct rainwater or moisture away from vulnerable areas, protecting the underlying structure from leaks, rot, and long-term damage.

## D-style gutter box

D-style gutter boxes, also known as D-profile gutters, are a popular rainwater drainage solution designed for efficient water flow and sleek architectural aesthetics. Shaped like the letter “D,” these gutter boxes combine the flat back and bottom of a box gutter with the curved front of traditional half-round gutters, offering both functionality and style.

Constructed from durable materials such as galvanized steel, aluminum, or color-coated metal, D-style gutters are corrosion-resistant and built to withstand heavy rainfall, UV exposure, and temperature variations. Their design ensures smooth water movement, minimizing clogs and overflows while blending seamlessly with both residential and commercial structures.
